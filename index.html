Overview

This project explores a cryptosystem built directly on the geometry of p-adic number fields, using Teichm?ller lifts and local truncation to realize structural quantum resistance.

This work proposes a novel cryptographic framework that fundamentally differs from conventional post-quantum cryptography. Instead of relying on Euclidean linear structures?such as lattices or error-correcting codes?this approach constructs a cryptosystem directly on the non-Archimedean geometry of p-adic fields, using the Teichm?ller lift and a locally-defined truncation operator as its central mathematical components. The goal is to establish structural quantum resistance: a form of quantum security arising not from computational hardness assumptions, but from the inherent geometric and algebraic properties of p-adic spaces.

The core idea is to exploit the stability of Teichm?ller fixed points. For each \( a \in \mathbb{F}_p \), the Teichm?ller lift \( \tau(a) \in \mathbb{Z}_p \) satisfies \( \tau(a)^p = \tau(a) \), forming a discrete and self-stabilizing set of fixed points within the p-adic integer ring. By mapping plaintext symbols to these fixed points, the cryptosystem operates within a digit-stable region of the p-adic space. This foundation is crucial for constructing predictable local behavior while maintaining strong global nonlinearity.

Building on this, we introduce the local truncation map \( W_{r,L} : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{F}_p^L \), which discards higher-order digits in a fundamentally irreversible manner. Unlike truncation in real-number systems, p-adic truncation does not approximate a value but instead collapses the infinite digit expansion along a tree-like p-adic hierarchy. This collapse acts as a one-way transformation: many distant p-adic values map to the same truncated output, while reconstructing the original point becomes exponentially hard with respect to digit depth. This property serves as the primary one-way primitive of the system.

Key Contributions

  • Teichm?ller-based key space: Cryptographic operations are defined on Teichm?ller fixed points in \( \mathbb{Z}_p \), ensuring digit stability and local self-consistency.
  • Local truncation as a one-way primitive: The map \( W_{r,L} \) irreversibly collapses p-adic expansions along a hierarchical tree, yielding an exponential barrier to inversion.
  • Non-linear block structure: The framework integrates non-linear diffusion using fractional-linear (projective) transformations over p-adic integers, enabling block-wise encryption with high sensitivity to local digit variations.
  • Beyond lattice-based PQC: Unlike lattice or code based cryptography, this approach does not rely on norm minimization, error terms, or vector-space structures. It opens a Ågthird directionÅh in post-quantum cryptography grounded in number-theoretic geometry.

Structural Quantum Resistance

A distinctive feature of this approach is that p-adic spaces do not support quantum-style linear interference or superposition in any natural computational sense. Non-Archimedean geometry enforces strong local closure: small perturbations remain confined within digit-stable regions, and linear combinations fail to extend globally in the way required by quantum algorithms such as Shor or Grover.

Consequently, the cryptosystem gains a new form of quantum robustness that does not primarily rely on computational hardness assumptions but on the structural incompatibility between quantum linearity and p-adic topology.
